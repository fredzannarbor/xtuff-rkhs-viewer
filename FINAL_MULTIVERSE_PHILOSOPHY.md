## RKHS Mathematical Framework

### Semantic Space (S)
- Real space of human knowledge
- Contains all semantically coherent entities
- Both locally-factual and non-locally-factual exist here
- RKHS structure with kernel similarity

### Our Multiverse Node (N₀)
- Subset of S containing locally-factual entities
- What we call "reality" or "truth"
- One point in infinite possibility space

### Alternate Nodes (N₁, N₂, ... N∞)
- Other subsets of S
- Each differs from N₀ by ≥1 entity
- May have significant overlap with N₀
- Infinite in number (likely)

### LLM as Generator
- Samples from semantic space S
- Generates elements from N₀ (locally-factual)
- Generates elements from N≠₀ (non-locally-factual)
- Can MIX elements from multiple nodes coherently
- Not analyzing errors, but creating possibilities

### RKHS Operations
- **Kernel similarity**: Distance between nodes
- **Fork operators**: Transitions from N₀ to N≠₀
- **Composition**: Mix entities from different nodes
- **Navigation**: Systematic exploration of multiverse space
- **Generation**: Use LLMs to create new coherent nodes
